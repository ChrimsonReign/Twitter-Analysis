Introduction¶

This is an analysis of the "WeRateDogs" twitter account's tweets. The main question I wanted to answer was if the dog raiting granted by this account effected the popularity of the tweet, as measured by number of favorites and number of retweets.

Analysis¶

The first thing I decided to look at was the distrobution of dog ratings as seen below.

As you can see, our data is heavily left scewed, with a long left tail and most of the data on the right side. The average dog rating is 1.01. Next, to answer my question of whether or not the popularity of a tweet is effected by the dog rating, we'll make some scatter plots of the dog rating against favorites and retweets.

This plot shows Favorites and Rating. I added some jitter to make interpretation easier, and I changed the alpha level to reduce overplotting. You can see an interesting trend here. As the rating gets higher, the tweets look like they gets more favorites. One thing we have to keep in mind is that our data was left skewed, and the average was 1.01, which means most of our tweets exist towards the right side of this graph, where the retweets and favorites are higher.

This plot shows a similar trend as the previous one, but not quite as pronounced.

You can see a correlation between favorites and rating, and a smaller correlation between retweets and rating. We'll need a better test though to know how big this impact really is. I calculated Pearson's R to test their correlation. The corrilation coefficient for favorites and rating is .305, and for retweets and ratings is .22. This indicates that the rating the dog receives from WeRateDogs has a moderate impact on how many favorites it gets, and a small impact on how many retweets it gets.
